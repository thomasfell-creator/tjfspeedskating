Basic Skating Position

The fundamental position/posture used by skaters to acheive speed and stability.

The purpose of basic position is to:

  • Lower your centre of mass
  • Maximise the amount of force your legs can produce
  • Improve balance and stability
  • Reduce air resistance
  • Build the foundation for cornering and acceleration

The below images gives a theoretical representation of the basic skating position, but it is importnat to note then when looked at in this manner, it is very easy to over simplify basic position.

While the image represents the typical angles we expect to see when holding basic position, we are all individuals with unique differences in body structure and movement patterns. Our leg lengths and body proportions will affect how we hold this position in practice.

Therefore, while it is important to recognise these 'angles' it can be far more practical to focus to the key steps taken to acheive basic position.
